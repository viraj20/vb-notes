# Golden circle — WHY / HOW / WHAT

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Source: [[../books/start-with-why|Start With Why]] (Sinek)

## The three layers

Three concentric circles. Communicating from the inside out (WHY → HOW → WHAT) inspires; from the outside in (WHAT → HOW → WHY) merely sells.

- **WHY** — "A WHY is just a belief." (loc 966) The cause the organization exists for, beyond profit.
- **HOW** — "HOWs are the actions you take to realize that belief." The working principles, the way the team operates.
- **WHAT** — "WHATs are the results of those actions — everything you say and do: your products, services, marketing, PR, culture and whom you hire." Authenticity happens here (loc 970).

## Why inversion matters

Most organizations communicate starting from WHAT — they lead with features, specs, benefits. Sinek's thesis: this is why they compete on price, quality, and service — commodity dimensions.

> "People don't buy WHAT you do, they buy WHY you do it." (loc 590)

The Apple example (loc 609, 627): Apple's products are the *tangible proof* of their WHY ("challenge the status quo"), not the reason customers buy.

## Values must be verbs

> "For values or guiding principles to be truly effective they have to be verbs. It's not 'integrity,' it's 'always do the right thing.' It's not 'innovation,' it's 'look at the problem from a different angle.'" (loc 957)

Noun-values are decoration; verb-values give a clear answer to "how do I act in this situation?"

## Clarity · Discipline · Consistency

Sinek's three properties for a sustained golden circle (loc 939–965):

- **Clarity of WHY** — do you actually know what you believe?
- **Discipline of HOW** — can the team keep doing the HOWs even when it's inconvenient?
- **Consistency of WHAT** — does the observable output match?

Drift at any layer erodes the next.
