# Flow state

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Source: [[../books/ikigai|Ikigai]] (Chapter 4)

## Definition

A state of complete involvement where time distortion, full attention, and unselfconscious engagement fuse. Csikszentmihalyi's term; Ikigai recasts it as the structural path to a worthwhile life.

## Preconditions

- **Task just outside comfort** — not too easy (boredom), not too hard (anxiety).
- **Clear objective** — known and held in the background, not obsessed over.
- **Single-focus** — one task; distractions excluded.
- **Low-friction start** — rituals signal the brain "now we begin."

## Techniques the note names

- **Pomodoro** — 25 min focused + 5 min break.
- **No tech at start/end of day** — protects the first and last flow windows.
- **Focus mode** on devices; monotasking OS settings.
- **Mindfulness practice** as training for sustained attention.
- **Microflow** — turn routine tasks into something you enjoy (making tea, walking a commute, tidying a desk) rather than treating them as downtime.

## The life claim

> "Happiest people spent more time in the state of flow. The more you flow the closer you will be to your ikigai."

Ikigai's thesis is that flow isn't just a productivity hack — it's the substrate on which meaning accumulates.
