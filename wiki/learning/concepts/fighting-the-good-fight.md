# Fighting the good fight

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

Petrus's teaching in [[../books/the-pilgrimage|The Pilgrimage]]: the worthwhile life is the one fought in the name of your dreams (loc 620) — not the one in which you win, and not the one in which you never have to fight.

## The three symptoms of killing your dreams (loc 626–634)

Coelho's diagnostic for a surrendered life:

1. **Lack of time.** "The busiest people I have known always have time enough to do everything. Those who do nothing are always tired and pay no attention to the little amount of work they are required to do."
   - *Inverted cliché.* "No time" is rarely a calendar fact; it is usually a priority fact.

2. **Certainty.** We start to think of ourselves as wise and fair and correct for asking so little of life. We see the "fire in the eyes of the warriors" but write them off as naïve.
   - *The resignation of the grown-up.* Safety becomes wisdom in retrospective self-narration.

3. **Peace — misread as maturity.** "Life becomes a Sunday afternoon; we ask for nothing grand. We put aside the fantasies of our youth. We are surprised when people our age still want something from life."
   - *The quietest death.* No dramatic defeat; just attrition.

## The reframe

> "Both attacking and fleeing are part of the fight. What isn't part of the fight is becoming paralyzed by fear." (loc 1456)

Engagement is the category that matters — not outcome. Defeat teaches; flight only declares the enemy's win (loc 2169).

## Related to other frames

- **Meaning as stance** — [[three-sources-of-meaning|Frankl]]: "Courage in difficult times." Same structural claim: meaning comes from how you *respond*, not what happens.
- **Flow as engagement** — [[flow-state|Flow]]: the engagement itself is the reward.
- **Inspired action** — [[../books/the-secret|Byrne]]: "Inspired action is when you are acting to receive." The inspired-action / fight distinction is real, and does not require Byrne's metaphysics.
- **Customer dev as search** — [[customer-development|Blank]]: "Focus on execution too early and you put yourself out of business." Blank's startup version of the same: the fight is finding the market, not executing a plan.
- **Bias to action** — [[../books/the-million-dollar-weekend|Noah Kagan]]: "Start before you feel ready."

## Applied

- **Careers.** A role that no longer requires you to fight for something produces the third symptom — peace mistaken for maturity.
- **On-call.** Incidents are the literal fight; running from on-call by avoiding ownership is the "flight" Coelho warns against.
