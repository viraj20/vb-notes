# Law of attraction

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

The claim, popularised by Rhonda Byrne's [[../books/the-secret|The Secret]], that thoughts emit a "frequency" which attracts matching circumstances into your life.

## The three-step mechanism Byrne proposes

1. **Ask** — get clear on what you want.
2. **Believe** — act, speak, think as if you've already received it.
3. **Receive** — feel the feeling now, to "tune" to the right frequency. (loc 904)

## What's defensible, stripped of physics language

Four mechanisms can be recovered as ordinary cognitive claims — no magical frequency required:

- **Attention selects action.** What you rehearse mentally is what you notice, plan for, and decide on. Thinking about X all day makes you more X-shaped. This is attention + availability heuristic, not physics.
- **Inspired action beats forced action.** Byrne's "inspired action" (loc 745) is motivation-quality framed as intrinsic vs. extrinsic pull — consistent with [[flow-state|flow]] and [[customer-development|customer development]]'s "build what people actually want."
- **Gratitude reframes attention.** "Gratitude brings your whole mind into closer harmony with the creative energies of the universe" (loc 955) → stripped: gratitude-practice shifts which features of your day you encode in memory.
- **Next-200-feet decision-making** (loc 776). You don't need the whole plan to start — just the next step. This is the same thing [[../books/the-million-dollar-weekend|Noah Kagan]] calls "bias to action."

## What's not supported

- "Universe as frequency field" has no physical referent. It's a metaphor presented as physics.
- **Victim-blaming.** The Secret's corollary that illness or poverty is caused by wrong-thinking is the position most criticised, and rightly so — it locates blame in victims of systemic or random misfortune.
- **Selection bias in the evidence.** Every Secret-book testimonial is from someone for whom it "worked"; the book is silent on the much larger population for whom it didn't.

## How to use the idea usefully

Keep the attention-steering, gratitude, inspired-action, and next-step claims. Discard the frequency metaphysics and the victim-blaming corollary.
