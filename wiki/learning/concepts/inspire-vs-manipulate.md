# Inspire vs manipulate

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Sources: [[../books/start-with-why|Sinek]], [[../books/predictably-irrational|Ariely]]

## The dichotomy

> "There are only two ways to influence human behavior: you can manipulate it or you can inspire it." — Sinek (loc 240)

- **Manipulation** — price drops, promotions, fear, aspirational messaging, peer pressure, novelty.
- **Inspiration** — a clearly articulated WHY that pulls people toward a belief they share.

## Manipulation works — for a while

Sinek's concession: for one-time transactions, manipulation is efficient. "For transactions that occur an average of once, carrots and sticks are the best way." (loc 464)

The problem is compounding. Each manipulation raises the baseline:

- **Price addiction** — "downward spiral of price addiction sets in" (loc 257); each discount requires the next one.
- **Fear as manipulator** — "arguably the most powerful manipulation" (loc 303), but fear degrades the culture it runs on.
- **Aspirational vs inspirational** — "you can get someone to buy a gym membership with an aspirational message, but to get them to go three days a week requires a bit of inspiration." (loc 332)

## Ariely reinforces it

- "MONEY, as it turns out, is very often the most expensive way to motivate people. Social norms are not only cheaper, but often more effective." — Ariely (loc 1245)
- Mixing [[social-vs-market-norms|social and market norms]] destroys the social norm; once converted, trust rarely returns (loc 1131).

## What inspires

Sinek's answer is the [[golden-circle|Golden Circle]] — WHY → HOW → WHAT, communicated inside-out. People are inspired when they encounter something they already believe articulated clearly by someone else.

> "We are drawn to leaders and organizations that are good at communicating what they believe. Their ability to make us feel like we belong … is part of what gives them the ability to inspire us." — Sinek (loc 786)

## For teams

- Hire already-motivated people; don't try to motivate the unmotivated (loc 1343).
- Give people something to work *toward*, not just *on* (loc 1419).
- Leader's role: create the environment where great ideas happen — not generate them all (loc 1420).
