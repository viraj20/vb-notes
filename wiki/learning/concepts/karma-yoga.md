# Karma yoga

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

The Gita's instruction, as rendered by [[../books/yogananda-bhagavad-gita|Yogananda]] (loc 693): work is performed with full dedication *and* with non-attachment to the fruit. Not detachment *from* work (that's quietism). Detachment from *outcome* — the thing the work is trying to produce.

> "To work without the inner peace of God is Hades; and to work with His joy ever bubbling through the soul is to carry a portable paradise within, wherever one goes." (loc 693)

De-sectarianise: the "inner peace" / "God" language is a tradition-specific container; the operational claim survives translation. You can be fully engaged with the work while not measuring your self-worth by the work's result.

## The mechanism

Why would the decoupling matter?

- **Outcomes are out of your control.** You can influence them, not determine them. Attaching identity to outcome makes your stability a function of other people's actions and of random variables.
- **Attachment changes the work.** When the result is load-bearing for who you are, you make worse decisions under pressure — you avoid uncomfortable feedback, you rush premature closure, you protect yourself instead of the work.
- **Sustainable effort requires the decoupling.** Marathon runs of bad quarters, long incident chains, multi-year projects — the only way to stay in them without burning out is to be in them without needing them to resolve in a specific way.

## Not the same as not caring

Karma yoga is not apathy. You care *about* the work; you do not make the work care about you. A karma-yogi surgeon performs the operation with full skill. If the patient dies, the surgeon grieves. What the surgeon doesn't do is *collapse* — because the identity that was doing the surgery wasn't betting on the patient's survival to remain intact.

The closest secular translation: [[three-sources-of-meaning|Frankl's]] "why" — meaning that isn't contingent on a specific outcome. Frankl's version is broader (meaning can come from work, love, or courage-in-suffering); karma-yoga's is narrower (a specific attitude toward action).

## Adjacent concepts

- [[flow-state|Flow state]] — shares the "work itself as its own reward" structure. Difference: flow requires challenge-to-skill match; karma yoga doesn't. You can karma-yoga a tedious task that offers no flow.
- [[samskaras|Samskaras]] — the Yogananda frame's companion concept. Every action leaves an impression. Karma yoga is the discipline of leaving the right kind of impression regardless of outcome.
- [[intrusive-thoughts-vs-self|Intrusive thoughts vs self]] — structurally parallel: "I am not my thoughts" in CBT; "I am not the fruits of my actions" in Gita. Both are defusion moves.
- [[golden-circle|Golden circle]] — Sinek's WHY is a secular framing that encourages the same non-attachment. You're acting from WHY, not from the expected WHAT.
- [[three-sources-of-meaning|Three sources of meaning]] — Frankl; broader container.

## For engineering contexts

### On-call practice

You cannot control whether the page fires at 3 AM. You can control how you walk into it. Karma-yoga on-call looks like: full attention to the alert, full care for the users, and a self that remains intact whether the incident resolves in 10 minutes or 10 hours.

The failure mode is attachment: "this incident reflects on me" produces rushed closure, thin post-mortems, and a next shift that you dread. The karma-yoga move doesn't make you careless; it makes you *durable*.

### Long migrations

A multi-quarter migration has many points where outcomes are uncertain. You can let each setback land personally ("this reflects on my judgment") or impersonally ("this is new information about the system"). The second attitude is not indifference — you still adjust the plan. But you don't hand the migration your self-respect as collateral.

### Performance reviews you don't fully control

Promotion decisions involve factors outside your execution. Karma-yoga here is: do the work that deserves promotion; do not tie your self-worth to the promotion landing. The first is in your control; the second isn't.

### When *not* to decouple

If the outcome genuinely matters beyond you (patient safety, financial fraud, user data loss), *some* attachment is load-bearing — it's the thing that makes you refuse to sign off on a bad release. Karma-yoga is not a license for indifference to consequences. It's a license to make good decisions *despite* consequences.

## The bounded version

A workable secular statement: **do the work with full care; hold the outcome with open hands.**

Almost everything else in the karma-yoga tradition is scaffolding for getting your nervous system to do that move consistently. The scaffolding is tradition-specific; the move is portable.
