# Customer development

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Source: [[../books/the-four-steps-to-the-epiphany|The Four Steps to the Epiphany]] (Blank)

## Definition

Blank's four-step process for validating a startup before executing on it. The core idea: a startup is a **search** for a scalable business model, not a smaller version of a big company. Execution comes after the search converges.

## The thesis in one line

> "Focusing on 'execution' will put you out of business. Instead, you need a 'learning and discovery' process so you can get the company to the point where you know what to execute." (loc 459)

## What's searched, in order

Blank's four steps (the book's spine):

1. **Customer Discovery** — do real people have this problem? Is our solution a must-have or a nice-to-have?
2. **Customer Validation** — will they pay? Can we find a repeatable, scalable sales process?
3. **Customer Creation** — now that we can sell, how do we build demand?
4. **Company Building** — only now scale into a full execution org.

## Diagnostic questions

From Blank's opening pages (loc 242, 276, 822):

- What problems does our product solve? Must-have?
- In B2B, who owns the problem? Who approves the purchase?
- How do we reach them (B2C)?
- How big is the problem? What's the average order size?
- How many customers do we need to be profitable?
- What do we do if our model turns out to be wrong?

These milestones measure *learning*, not shipped features.

## Counter-intuitive corollaries

- **Customer meetings are not requirements gathering** — "your initial purpose in meeting customers is not to gather feature requests so you can change the product; it is to find customers for the product you are already building." (loc 907)
- **Markets before products** — "the company must prove a market could exist, verify someone would pay real dollars for the solutions the company envisions, and then go out and create the market." (loc 477)
