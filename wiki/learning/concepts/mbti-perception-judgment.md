# MBTI perception vs judgment

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

The usable core of Myers's framework ([[../books/gifts-differing|Gifts Differing]]), separated from the type-inventory that MBTI is famous and unreliable for. Two axes describe two basic cognitive activities; two more describe the attitudes that shape them.

The page keeps the *vocabulary* — it is a good descriptive handle — and explicitly rejects the *instrument* — the MBTI questionnaire produces unreliable classifications.

## The four axes

### Perception (how you gather information)

- **Sensing (S)** — directly through the five senses; interested in actualities; values what is.
- **Intuition (N)** — indirectly via the unconscious, adding associations to incoming perception; interested in possibilities; values what could be.

### Judgment (how you reach conclusions)

- **Thinking (T)** — evaluates via true/false.
- **Feeling (F)** — evaluates via agreeable/disagreeable (which is not the same as emotional — it's a values-based rather than logic-based evaluation).

### Attitude (energy direction)

- **Extraversion (E)** — energy outward, toward people and objects.
- **Introversion (I)** — energy inward, toward ideas and concepts. Myers emphasises (loc 448) that a cardinal precaution with introverts is not to assume they've revealed what matters from ordinary contact.

### Lifestyle (mode preference)

- **Judging (J)** — prefers to run life in judgment-mode; wants decisions made, closure reached.
- **Perceiving (P)** — prefers to run life in perception-mode; wants evidence to keep accumulating, closure postponed.

## Myers's load-bearing insight

Not the types. This (loc 1078, 1085):

> "It takes judgment (either thinking or feeling will do) to give continuity of purpose and supply a standard by which to criticize and govern one's own actions."

> "It takes perception (either sensing or intuition will do) to supply understanding, open-mindedness, and the first-hand knowledge of life needed to keep judgment itself from being blind."

Perception and judgment are *complementary operations* that have to take turns. This is the single most portable claim in the book. An engineer who is all perception and no judgment never decides. One who is all judgment and no perception decides on stale inputs.

## Reactions to change (loc 796)

Myers's sketch of how the four perception/judgment combinations tend to receive a proposed change:

- **Thinking types** — criticize, analyze, or organize it.
- **Feeling types** — champion, protest, or mitigate it.
- **Sensing types** — enjoy, use, or good-naturedly put up with it.
- **Intuitive types** — try to change it further.

A team-shape lens. Use when staffing a major migration or redesign: all four reactions are needed. Without critics the plan ships weak; without champions it doesn't ship at all; without absorbers adoption is zero; without re-tinkerers the second version never arrives.

## Why the inventory fails

The MBTI questionnaire (the 16-type test) has poor test-retest reliability — roughly 50% of takers get a different type on a re-test within weeks. The underlying personality science has moved to Big Five / OCEAN, which is more predictive and more stable.

Use implications:

1. **Never use MBTI results for hiring.** Illegal in several jurisdictions; unwise everywhere.
2. **Don't use it for team composition decisions.** The type assignment is too unstable to design around.
3. **Don't collapse people to their 4-letter code.** Myers explicitly models dominant-function hierarchies (loc 378) — the ordering matters — and the letter tests don't capture it.
4. **Do keep the vocabulary.** "This is a perception problem, not a judgment problem" is a real useful sentence.

## Myers vs other taxonomies

- [[hemispheric-attention|Hemispheric attention]] — McGilchrist's left/right modes. Overlapping terrain: perception ≈ right-mode broad vigilance, judgment ≈ left-mode narrow focus. Not isomorphic; both frames have things the other doesn't.
- **Big Five / OCEAN** — more psychometrically valid but less vocabulary-useful in conversation. The two frames can coexist: Big Five for measurement, Myers for shared language.
- [[three-sources-of-meaning|Frankl]] — orthogonal axis (meaning-type), not a competitor.

## For engineering contexts

- **1:1 prep.** Am I going in to *perceive* (listen, gather, update my model) or to *judge* (decide, direct, close)? Myers's frame forces the question.
- **Incident retro.** Same: the first pass should be perception-heavy (what happened, who saw what, what didn't fit). The second is judgment-heavy (root cause, action items). Mixing them compresses evidence.
- **Team staffing.** The reactions-to-change signature is the only team-composition move I'd trust from the MBTI corpus — and I'd trust the *shape* of the signature, not the measurement.
