# The Great Gatsby (Fitzgerald)

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

F. Scott Fitzgerald's 1925 novel. VB captured 1 highlight; this is a **placeholder**.

## What was captured

> "Whenever you feel like criticizing any one, just remember that all the people in this world haven't had the advantages that you've had." (loc 17)

Nick Carraway's father's advice — the opening-page epigraph that becomes the book's implicit ethical framework. Nick's withholding-judgment habit is what makes him a credible narrator of Gatsby's myth; it's also what makes him complicit in it.

## Re-read pending

The book's thematic core — the American Dream as hollow spectacle, the green light as desire-object, Daisy as the prize that was never really what was being pursued — is the material worth a re-read. See [[the-millionaire-fastlane|DeMarco's]] "slowlane" critique for the same argument, stripped of romance.
