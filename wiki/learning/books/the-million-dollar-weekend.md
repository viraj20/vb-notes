# The Million Dollar Weekend (Sethi / Ravikant-style playbook)

- Source: personal reading notes (captured 2024-08-15)

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Related: [[the-millionaire-fastlane|Millionaire Fastlane]]

## Core argument

Start before you feel ready. Make a game of rejection until asking feels boring. A "freedom number" turns vague ambitions into a concrete target.

## Key takeaways

- **Treat it as an experiment** — lowers stakes, keeps learning loops short.
- **Freedom number** — VB's note: ₹300,000 (/mo? /yr? — the note is ambiguous; re-check on next pass).
- **Focus on the first dollar** — the first paying customer validates demand in a way no market research can.
- **Just start** — beat planning paralysis with action.
- **Love rejections, set rejection goals** — asking is a muscle; rejection is the reps.
- **The coffee challenge** — ask 10% off your coffee order; cheap practice for "the ask."
- **Unlimited upside to asking** — the downside is "no" (baseline); the upside is asymmetric.
