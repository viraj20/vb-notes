# Pride and Prejudice (Austen)

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

Jane Austen's 1813 novel of manners. VB captured only 4 highlights; this is a **placeholder**.

## What was captured

- **The opening line** (loc 44) — "It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife." Ironic framing; the novel inverts it.
- **On candour** (loc 216) — "To be candid without ostentation or design — to take the good of everybody's character and make it still better, and say nothing of the bad — belongs to you."
- **On concealing affection** (loc 308) — "If a woman conceals her affection with the same skill from the object of it, she may lose the opportunity of fixing him." (i.e., restraint has a cost.)
- **On marriage and knowledge** (loc 330) — "Happiness in marriage is entirely a matter of chance… it is better to know as little as possible of the defects of the person with whom you are to pass your life." (Sardonic; Charlotte Lucas's pragmatic view, not Austen's endorsement.)
