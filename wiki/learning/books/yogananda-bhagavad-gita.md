# God Talks with Arjuna: The Bhagavad Gita — Paramahansa Yogananda

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

Yogananda's two-volume commentary on the Gita, framed explicitly as yogic psychology rather than religious text. The 9 captured highlights cluster around four portable concepts — only the religious-cosmology ones (samadhi, astral/causal bodies) don't port outside the tradition.

## The four load-bearing ideas

### 1. Karma yoga — duty without attachment to fruit

Yogananda, loc 693:

> "Man should so train his mind by constant meditation that he can perform the necessary dutiful actions of his daily life and still maintain the consciousness of God within. That is the example set by Krishna's life. Sri Krishna's message in the Bhagavad Gita is the perfect answer for the modern age, and any age: Yoga of dutiful action, of nonattachment, and of meditation for God-realization. To work without the inner peace of God is Hades; and to work with His joy ever bubbling through the soul is to carry a portable paradise within, wherever one goes."

The claim: you can do the work *and* remain unattached to its fruit. Not detachment-from-work (quietism); detachment-from-outcome. See [[../concepts/karma-yoga|karma yoga]] for the concept developed.

The portable-paradise-within phrase maps onto [[../concepts/flow-state|flow]] but is distinct: flow requires the activity's challenge to match your skill; karma-yoga doesn't require the match — it requires the *attitude*.

### 2. Samskaras — impressions left by thought and action

Loc 1838:

> "A thought or physical act once performed does not cease to be, but remains in the consciousness in a more subtle or 'melted' form as an impression of that gross expression of thought or action. These impressions are called samskaras."

Same mechanism that [[walton-emotional-intelligence|Walton]] (loc 167) calls "behavioural templates" in the Western-psychology register. See [[../concepts/samskaras|samskaras]] for the concept page — the engineering parallel is muscle memory built over months of on-call; the template fires whether the conscious mind agrees or not.

### 3. The body-as-kingdom diagnostic

Loc 1072:

> "How King Soul rules over his bodily kingdom, loses and then regains it, is the essence of the Gita."

And loc 993:

> "If the intelligence is governed by buddhi, the pure discriminative power, the senses are controlled; if the intelligence is ruled by material desires, the senses are wild and unruly."

The metaphor is a diagnostic: when you lose to a craving or a reactive emotion, King Soul temporarily loses the kingdom. Don't treat this as failure; treat it as data about what you then do to regain it. Adjacent to [[../concepts/intrusive-thoughts-vs-self|intrusive thoughts vs self]] — you are not the usurper.

### 4. Identification with Changelessness

Loc 1558:

> "Identification of the consciousness with the alternating waves of change is known as restlessness; identity with Changelessness is calmness."

The claim: calm is not the absence of change but the identification with what doesn't change. In CBT terms, this is defusion from transient content — see [[../concepts/intrusive-thoughts-vs-self|intrusive thoughts vs self]]. The Gita's frame is older and more sweeping; Gilbert's/Green's frame is narrower and more operational.

## What to skip (or at least bracket)

- The **astral / causal body** cosmology (loc 591) — a tradition-specific metaphysics. Accept or reject; don't try to port.
- The **samadhi → ever-new Bliss** language (loc 430) — also tradition-specific. The experiential-report side is real (deep meditators report this); the metaphysical claim is not falsifiable, see [[../concepts/falsifiability|falsifiability]].
- The **divine love vs carnal desire** framing (loc 646) — a common ascetic trope; Gilbert's [[eat-pray-love|Eat Pray Love]] and [[mans-search-for-meaning|Frankl]]'s love-as-meaning arrive at richer positions without requiring the asceticism.

## For engineering contexts

### On-call as karma yoga practice

On-call pages you whether you want them or not. You cannot control the fruit of the work (the alert resolving cleanly; the next sprint not having another one). You *can* control the attitude with which you walk into the page. Karma-yoga is the discipline of walking in without the attachment that turns every page into a personal affront.

### Samskaras and unchecked toil

Every time you paper over a bad alert instead of fixing it, the template sets a little deeper. Three months in, the "acknowledge and move on" behavior fires automatically. [[../concepts/toil|Toil]] is not just work-that-scales; it is work that builds the wrong samskara if left unchecked.

### Identification with Changelessness, incident edition

During a live incident, there is tremendous pressure to *be* the panic — to merge identity with the alert graph. The teams that execute best are the ones whose ICs have identified with something steadier. Sometimes that's the runbook; sometimes the team; sometimes the user. But the merge-with-panic identification loses the kingdom.
