# Predictably Irrational (Ariely)

- Author: Dan Ariely
- ASIN: B002RI9QJE
- Source: Kindle highlights (14 annotations)

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Related: [[the-millionaire-fastlane|Millionaire Fastlane]] · [[start-with-why|Start With Why]] · [[../../finances/index|Finances MOC]]

## Core argument

Human decision-making is irrational in *predictable* ways. Behavioral economics isn't a collection of quirks — it's a structure you can design around, whether you're pricing a product, designing a team incentive, or trying not to blow up your own finances.

## Key ideas extracted

- **Relativity of value** — "humans rarely choose things in absolute terms … we focus on the relative advantage of one thing over another" (loc 200). Price a product by the anchor you offer, not by intrinsic worth.
- **Context precedes preference** — "most people don't know what they want unless they see it in context." (loc 209) Context is the product.
- **Comparability bias** — we compare easily comparable things and avoid the hard comparisons (loc 260). This is why UX designers place a "bad" middle option.
- **Anchoring via memory** — "sensitivity we show to price changes might in fact be largely a result of our memory for the prices we have paid in the past and our desire for coherence with our past decisions." (loc 730) Connects to [[the-millionaire-fastlane|DeMarco's]] argument that the Sidewalk lifestyle anchors permanently.
- **Social norms vs market norms** — mixing the two is destructive. "Once a social norm is trumped by a market norm, it will rarely return." (loc 1131). See [[../concepts/social-vs-market-norms|Social vs market norms]].
- **Money is expensive motivation** — "MONEY, AS IT turns out, is very often the most expensive way to motivate people. Social norms are not only cheaper, but often more effective as well." (loc 1245) Directly relevant to team design; complements [[start-with-why|Sinek's]] view on inspiration.
- **Loss aversion** — "we focus on what we may lose, rather than what we may gain." (loc 1822) Explains irrational attachment to sunk assets.
- **Reputation as regulator** — "people almost always depends upon the favour and good opinion of their neighbours … honesty is always the best policy, holds … perfectly true." (loc 2680) Ties to [[../concepts/inspire-vs-manipulate|inspire vs manipulate]].
- **Incentives distort understanding** — "It is difficult to get a man to understand something when his salary depends upon his not understanding it." (loc 3005) Upton Sinclair via Ariely.
