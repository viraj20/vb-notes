# To Kill A Mockingbird

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

Harper Lee (1960). Depression-era Alabama courtroom novel; Atticus Finch defending Tom Robinson. 17 highlights — sparse but every one load-bearing.

## Atticus's ethical core

Three interlocking claims. All three hold up outside the book.

### Perspective-taking (loc 507)

> "You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view — until you climb into his skin and walk around in it."

See [[../concepts/perspective-taking|Perspective-taking]] for the generalised concept. Runs in parallel to Carnegie's "talk about what the other person is interested in" and Gray's [[../concepts/mr-fix-it-trap|Mr. Fix-It trap]] (asking "do you want me to listen?" is perspective-taking operationalised).

### Conscience over majority (loc 1863)

> "Before I can live with other folks I've got to live with myself. The one thing that doesn't abide by majority rule is a person's conscience."

The structural claim matters beyond the novel: group consensus is not a check on conscience; conscience is a check on group consensus. Orwell's [[animal-farm|Animal Farm]] shows the inverse — what happens when majority-rule becomes the only ethical instrument.

### Real courage (loc 1993)

> "I wanted you to see what real courage is, instead of getting the idea that courage is a man with a gun in his hand. It's when you know you're licked before you begin but you begin anyway and you see it through no matter what. You rarely win, but sometimes you do."

Same structural shape as Coelho's [[../concepts/fighting-the-good-fight|Fighting the good fight]] and Frankl's third source of meaning ([[../concepts/three-sources-of-meaning|Three sources of meaning]]) — courage-in-suffering. Lee names it without the mythology.

## Smaller gems

- **Delete the adjectives** (loc 1039) — Scout's journalistic heuristic. Engineering parallel: a post-mortem written without adjectives is more useful than one dressed in regret.
- **Talent without pride** (loc 1748) — "people in their right minds never take pride in their talents." The humility cuts against the bottom half of [[the-48-laws-of-power|The 48 Laws of Power]].
- **Confirmation bias, plain** (loc 3049) — "People generally see what they look for, and hear what they listen for." Folk version of what the cognitive-science literature formalises.
- **Talk about their interests** (loc 2693) — "talk to people about what they were interested in, not about what you were interested in." Carnegie's headline rule, Atticus's version.

## Other notable passages

Atticus's courage has a quieter register too — a father who "hated guns and had never been to any wars" was still "the bravest man who ever lived" (loc 1783). Scout's verdict frames bravery as a civic posture rather than a performance; the same man who refuses the rifle is the one standing in the courthouse. Paired with loc 1993, it closes the loop: real courage is the slow kind.

The novel's women are not incidental to its moral arithmetic. Maycomb's ladies "arrange, advise, caution, and warn" (loc 2254) — the small-town ritual machinery — while Mayella Ewell, "because her desires were stronger than the code she was breaking, persisted in breaking it" (loc 3598). Lee lets the code and the breach sit in the same paragraph without editorialising; Scout notices both and files them away.

Childhood ends not at a moment but at a pace the child sets. Jem, after the trial, was "storing it away for a while, until enough time passed" before he could "think about it and sort things out" (loc 4380). That deferral is the book's model of moral reckoning — unglamorous, delayed, private. The counter-note is the neighbour's flat assessment of Ewells: the sort you "have to shoot before you can say hidy to 'em" and even then "ain't worth the bullet it takes to shoot 'em" (loc 4760) — Maycomb's appetite for verdicts without process, the thing Atticus spends the book refusing.

## For engineering contexts

- **Perspective-taking in incident review.** "Why would a reasonable engineer have done this?" is Atticus asking Scout to climb into their skin. Blameless review is not Lee-grade moral seriousness, but it uses the same tool.
- **Conscience over consensus on risk.** If the whole room thinks a deploy is safe and you don't, conscience beats majority. Atticus in the SRE seat.
- **"Real courage" as on-call ethic.** You know the page is bad, you know the fix is painful, you know you're tired. Start anyway.
