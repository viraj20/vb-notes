# And Then There Were None (Christie)

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

Agatha Christie's 1939 closed-room murder mystery. VB captured 3 character-introduction highlights; this is a **placeholder**. The book's craft lesson — ten strangers on an island, picked off one by one, culminating in a reveal that broke the "detective must survive" convention — is the reason it's the best-selling mystery of all time.

## What was captured

- **Mr. Justice Wargrave** (loc 55) — retired judge, first-class smoker.
- **Vera Claythorne** (loc 76) — third-class carriage, eyes shut.
- **Philip Lombard** (loc 101) — summing up a stranger "in a mere flash of his quick-moving eyes."

VB's highlights are the opening character establishment shots — Christie's compact POV introductions, no thematic content captured.
