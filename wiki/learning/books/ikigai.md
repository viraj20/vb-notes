# Ikigai

- Source: personal reading notes (2023-05-17, manual capture)

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]] · Related: [[sophies-world|Sophie's World]] · [[../concepts/flow-state|Flow state]]

## Core argument

Ikigai — "the reason we get up in the morning" — emerges from the intersection of purpose, health, social ties, and flow. Morita therapy suggests accepting emotions while acting: action changes feeling, not the reverse.

## Key takeaways

- **The four pillars** — diet, exercise, finding a purpose, forming social ties.
- **Reduce stress** — a sound mind and a sound body; expose yourself to change.
- **Logotherapy over analysis** — discover meaning rather than create it; Frankl: "He who has a why can live with almost any how."
- **Act first, feel later** — Morita therapy: teach patients to accept emotions without controlling them; feelings change as a result of actions, not the reverse.
- **[[../concepts/flow-state|Flow state]]** — complete involvement, time distortion, clear goal, single task, slight challenge above comfort.
- **Pomodoro pattern** — 25 min focused + 5 min break; no tech at start/end of day; focus mode.
- **Sophisticated simplicity** — attention to detail, pleasure in routine work.
- **Microflow** — turn routine tasks into something enjoyed; the happiest people spend more time in flow.
