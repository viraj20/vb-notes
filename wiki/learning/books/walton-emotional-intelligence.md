# A Practical Guide to Emotional Intelligence — David Walton

Parent: [[../index|Learning MOC]]

**Thin page, one load-bearing fragment.** Only 4 highlights captured, three of them near-fragments. One is worth pulling into the wiki: the **behavioural templates** observation (loc 167).

## The one useful fragment

Walton, loc 167:

> "building up behavioural templates, which are stored in your brain and used for…"

The highlight is cut off, but the concept — that repeated behaviour under emotional context stores a template that fires automatically next time — is the Western-psychology version of Yogananda's [[../concepts/samskaras|samskaras]] (loc 1838 in [[yogananda-bhagavad-gita|Bhagavad Gita]]: "A thought or physical act once performed does not cease to be, but remains in the consciousness in a more subtle or 'melted' form as an impression"). Both point at the same mechanism.

Engineering analogue: on-call response to pages over months builds a behavioural template. The first 20 middle-of-the-night alerts are cognitively expensive; by the 100th, the template fires, and you're triaging before you're fully awake. Same mechanism builds *bad* templates — "every alert is ignorable" is a template, and it's the one that bites.
