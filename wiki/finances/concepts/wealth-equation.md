# Wealth equation

Parent: [[../index|Finances MOC]] · Source: [[../../learning/books/the-millionaire-fastlane|The Millionaire Fastlane]]

## Three equations, three roadmaps

DeMarco shows that each "roadmap" is implicitly governed by a math equation that dictates its destination.

### Sidewalk → poverty

```
wealth = income + debt
```

Lifestyle is pegged to paycheck and supplemented with credit. When income disappears, the illusion disappears (loc 892, 897). High earners can be Sidewalkers — rappers, athletes, actors whose bankruptcies make news (loc 900).

### Slowlane → mediocrity

```
wealth = (job × 50 years) + (market returns × compounding)
```

Trades today's time for a distant, uncertain retirement. Low leverage, low control, unchangeable variables.

### Fastlane → wealth

```
wealth = profit + asset value
profit = units sold × unit profit
asset value = net profit × industry multiple
```

Both variables on both equations are **controllable** (loc 952-era material). More units, higher margins, better multiples — all levers the owner pulls.

## Why the equation is the roadmap

> "To force change, change must come from your beliefs, and your roadmap circumscribes those beliefs." — DeMarco (loc 769)

The equation isn't a description of outcome — it's the grammar of the decisions you make. If your implicit equation is `wealth = income + debt`, your daily choices will reliably produce a Sidewalker life regardless of salary.
