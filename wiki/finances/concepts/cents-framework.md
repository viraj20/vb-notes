# CENTS framework

Parent: [[../index|Finances MOC]] · Source: [[../../learning/books/the-millionaire-fastlane|The Millionaire Fastlane]]

## The five commandments

DeMarco's filter for whether a business has Fastlane potential. All five should be present; missing any one makes the venture a job masquerading as a business.

- **C — Control** — you control the product, brand, operations, and customer relationship. No landlord (platform, franchise, employer) can revoke your access.
- **E — Entry** — entry barriers exist. Low-barrier businesses get commoditized; the easier it was to get in, the less it's worth.
- **N — Need** — solves a real need, not a want you invented. Product-market fit, in a less trendy accent.
- **T — Time** — income decouples from your hours. The business survives your absence.
- **S — Scale** — the ceiling is enormous. Revenue isn't bounded by geography or per-unit time.

## Why it's a filter, not a blueprint

CENTS is negative — it rules things out. Many "businesses" fail one or more:

- Consulting → fails **T** (paid per hour).
- Franchise → fails **C** (franchisor controls).
- Affiliate site → fails **E** (trivial entry) and often **C** (platform-dependent).
- Local service → fails **S** (geographically capped).

Passing CENTS doesn't guarantee success — it guarantees you're playing on a board where success is possible.

## Unlimited leverage

CENTS businesses have controllable variables in the wealth equation:

```
wealth = profit + asset value
profit = units sold × unit profit
asset value = net profit × industry multiple
```

In a job, neither side is meaningfully controllable. In a CENTS business, both are.
