# Wealth trinity — family, fitness, freedom

Parent: [[../index|Finances MOC]] · Source: [[../../learning/books/the-millionaire-fastlane|The Millionaire Fastlane]]

## Definition

DeMarco's reframe of "wealth": not material possessions, but three interlocking components.

- **Family** — the quality of relationships.
- **Fitness** — health, physical and mental.
- **Freedom** — agency over time and choice.

> "Wealth is not authored by material possessions, money, or 'stuff,' but by what I call the three fundamental F's: family (relationships), fitness (health), and freedom (choice)." — DeMarco (loc 952)

## Why it matters

The trinity is interdependent. Destroying **freedom** (e.g. by chaining yourself to a 60-hour job to fund an unaffordable lifestyle) also attacks fitness (no time to exercise) and family (no time to be present). DeMarco calls this **faux wealth** — chasing the appearance of wealth *destroys* actual wealth (loc 995, 1011).

## The corrupted default

Society equips people with a corrupted definition:

> "'Wealth' is a chauffeur-driven Rolls Royce … watches that cost more than most cars." (loc 938)

Accepting this default silently redirects decisions to erode the trinity. DeMarco's prescription: redefine wealth in terms of the three F's, then run every major financial choice against it.

## Money's role

Money doesn't buy happiness on its own, but it **buys the freedom** that makes family and fitness attainable (loc 1072):

- Freedom to watch kids grow up.
- Freedom to pursue the weird dreams.
- Freedom to make a difference.
- Freedom to build relationships.
- Freedom to do what you love without needing it to pay.
