There is a fundamental opposition between the two attitudes. In order to come to a conclusion, people use the judging attitude and have to shut off perception for the time being. All the evidence is in, and anything more is irrelevant and immaterial. The time has come to arrive at a verdict. Conversely, in the perceptive attitude people shut off judgment. Not all the evidence is in; new developments will occur. It is much too soon to do anything irrevocable. This preference makes the difference between the judging people, who order their lives, and the perceptive people, who just live them. — location: [346](kindle://book?action=open&asin=B01HPVHGP8&location=346) ^ref-3004
