- EPS
	- Earnings per share=Net Income / Total outstanding shares
	- Debt is not considered
	- Increasing EPS is good
- P/E ratio
	- Market price / EPS
	- IF P/E is 50 it means that investors are ready to invest 50 rupees to earn 1 rupees
	- High P/E ratio indicates the company can grow faster
- P/C Ratio
- Book Value
	- BV = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
	- BV/Share = BV / No. of outstanding shares
	- Does not consider intangible assets like patents, goodwill, brand value
	- Should not be used for analysis of industries like IT and services coz they have intangible assets
	- Used for manufacturing, asset heavy and automobile engineering companies
- PB Ratio 
	- Market Price per share / Book Value per share
	- Tells whether a stock in under valued or over valued
- Price To Sales Ratio
	- sales per share / number of shares
- Dividend
	- Calculated on FV
	- Growth stage companies avoid giving dividends
- Div Yields
	- (Div Per Share / Market price per share) * 100
	- Money investors get as dividend for every 100 rupees invested
- Market lot
- Deliverables
	- % of shares that are bought for delivery

- Debt to Equity Ratio 
	- Ideal is 1
	- Check for trends in this ratio

- Interest Coverage Ratio
	- profit / interest
	  
- ROE Return on Equity
	- Total Profit / Total Equity

- Current Ratio 
	- Current Assets / Current Liability
	- 1.5 to 2 could change based on industry
